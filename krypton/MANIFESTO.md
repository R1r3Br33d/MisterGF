## How It Works

### The Hub
Every time you log in, you see:
- **Trending Topics** - What's popular NOW (actual engagement, not manipulation)
- **By Sector** - Music, Art, Tech, Writing, Local News, Politics, etc.
- **Community Spotlight** - Most respected creators this week
- **Recent Activity** - What your friends and followed creators posted

### Individual Blogs
- Each user gets a customizable blog
- Post any type of content: writing, music, art, videos, photography
- Your followers see your posts
- Comments create conversation
- That's it. No engagement manipulation.

### Discovery Through Recommendations
Instead of an algorithm showing you random content:
- **Real people mention what they love** in their posts and comments
- **Mentions create visibility** - content gets seen based on human enthusiasm
- **Quality earns rewards** - both the recommender and creator earn reputation
- **Authenticity is scored** - genuine recommendations are weighted higher

### Reputation (The Recommendation Economy)

```
Example:
@alice posts: "@bob's new album absolutely blew my mind"

✓ Krypton detects genuine mention
✓ Scores authenticity (context, engagement, history)
✓ Amplifies in feed
✓ @alice earns: 'great recommender' points
✓ @bob earns: influence points + potential revenue
✓ Both have their reputation strengthened
```

**Reputation = Social Currency**
- High reputation = your recommendations carry weight
- Your posts get seen more
- You earn first when patronage pools distribute
- Your voice matters in community decisions

## Community Governance

### How Decisions Get Made

**Every major change goes to community vote:**

1. **Proposal Period** (1 week)
   - Anyone can propose changes
   - Public discussion
   - Community feedback

2. **Voting Period** (1 week)
   - All users vote (1 person = 1 vote)
   - Transparent vote counting
   - Supermajority required (66%)

3. **Implementation** (if approved)
   - Changes take effect
   - Documented publicly
   - Can be reversed if harmful

### Early Governance Council
- 7 elected community representatives
- Serve 6-month terms
- Quarterly community reviews
- Tie-breaking authority only
- Term limits (max 2 terms)

### Content Policy (Community Decided)
Krypton itself won't dictate content rules. Instead:
- Community votes on moderation approach
- Sectors can have their own guidelines
- Appeal process transparent
- No hidden algorithms suppressing speech
